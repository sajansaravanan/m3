NGC 604: A Stellar Nursery

NGC 604 is one of the largest and most active star-forming regions in the Triangulum Galaxy (M33).

It is located approximately 3 million light-years away from Earth and is a bright region of ionized hydrogen.

The nebula is home to hundreds of young, massive stars that illuminate the surrounding gas and dust.

Recent observations by NASA's James Webb Space Telescope have revealed intricate details about its structure and composition.

NGC 604 serves as a valuable laboratory for studying the processes of star formation and evolution.
